⚠ THE PROBLEM

COMMUNICATION
SHOULDN'T BE
A BARRIER.

Deaf and mute individuals are effectively excluded from the modern video calling experience — silenced by platforms that were never designed for them.

Most video conferencing tools — Google Meet, Zoom, Teams — are built entirely around voice. There is no native support for sign language, and no way for a deaf-mute person to communicate naturally without an interpreter present.

This creates an invisible wall: social isolation during group calls, dependency on third parties for basic communication, and a daily reminder that mainstream technology simply wasn't built with them in mind.

The gap between deaf-mute users and hearing users isn't just technical — it's deeply human. Mute Verse exists to close that gap, one sign at a time.

⚙ HOW IT WORKS

THREE STEPS
TO CONNECTION.

01 ⬇
DOWNLOAD & LAUNCH
Clone the GitHub repo, install Python dependencies, and run ui.py. No cloud account. No subscription. Runs entirely on your machine.
02 📹
OPEN GOOGLE MEET
Start or join any Meet call. Mute Verse automatically finds the active window and begins scanning it for hand gestures in real time.
03 💬
SIGN & BE HEARD
Perform an ASL gesture. A smooth subtitle overlay appears on-screen instantly — visible to everyone in the call. No interpreter needed.
